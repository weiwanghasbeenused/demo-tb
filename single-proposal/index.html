We will explore how the message of America as land of opportunity can be expressed through the projection of future landscapes, transitioning from a culture of extraction to one of innovation and renewal to maintain collective prosperity.

New visual and physical connections will include a land bridge, forming an axis between the Jefferson Memorial and the White House. A jetty in the Potomac River off the Lincoln Memorial will house the relocated memorial to Martin Luther King, Jr., connecting national struggles for civil rights over time.

The creation of new wetlands and a green wall will, like a sponge, absorb rising waters. Sedimentation will create tidal flats reducing runoff, storm surge, and flooding. These new features offer soft and dynamic edges.

Changes will protect both natural and made-made features from the effects of flooding; to secure their futures, the memorials to Roosevelt and King as well as the cherry trees will be moved and a berm will be built.
